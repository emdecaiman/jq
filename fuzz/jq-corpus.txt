I split the corpus by jq’s actual fuzzing surfaces instead of shipping one mixed seed directory. The bundle has separate inputs for `jq_fuzz_compile`, `jq_fuzz_execute`, `jq_fuzz_fixed`, `jq_fuzz_load_file`, `jq_fuzz_parse`, `jq_fuzz_parse_extended`, and `jq_fuzz_parse_stream`. The `execute/` seeds are delimiter-packed for LLVM `FuzzedDataProvider::ConsumeRandomLengthString()`, `fixed/` seeds are packed for its two-string-plus-selector layout, and `parse_extended/` seeds begin with jq’s parse/print flag words from `jv.h`. ([GitHub][1])

The zip currently contains 755 `compile` seeds, 736 `cli_filters`, 958 `cli_json`, 128 `execute`, 241 `fixed`, 970 each for `parse`/`parse_stream`/`load_file`, 85 `parse_extended`, 12 `module_filters`, 8 module fixture files, and 2 optional dictionaries. I also included a module fixture tree plus CLI-ready module seeds so you can fuzz `import`/`include` paths separately.

For the seed material, I pulled from jq’s own test corpus and `.jq` files, and I added synthetic edge cases for BOM-prefixed JSON, deep nesting, JSON sequences, multi-root inputs, malformed JSON, and streaming-heavy compound documents. That lines up with jq’s own discussion of AFL seeding from test `.jq` files plus `tests/torture/input0.json`, jq’s documented `--stream` / `--stream-errors` / `--seq` modes, and AFL++ guidance to start from diverse real inputs, then deduplicate/minimize with `afl-cmin`; I removed exact duplicates, but I did not run coverage-based minimization because AFL++ and instrumented jq targets were not available in this container. ([GitHub][2])
